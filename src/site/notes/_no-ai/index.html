essay **AI art is not _generative art_** (clickbait title). While the technical definition says that one is a subset of the other, I think it’s useful to distinguish between these two categories. **Why I’m writing this in the first place —** Starting 2022, the term “generative art” has been progressively becoming synonymous with art produced by AI text-to-image systems. As a consumer and producer of “generative art”, it’s becoming a bit annoying to browse generative art content on the internet. Whether through tags like `#generativeart` or communities like `r/generative`, they are being flooded with AI-generated images which I’m not interested in. End rant. At this point you may be asking, what’s the difference anyway? Well, that’s the point of this post. It’s kinda like animals vs humans. While technically & scientifically, humans are animals; in daily life “animals” usually mean non-human animals, as in “animal lover” and “animal rights”. Imagine browsing `r/AnimalsBeingGeniuses` then seeing only humans doing mundane things like reading or writing. I digress. Let’s look at the differences in different angles, starting with _lineages_. ## Lineages Tracing the history of each practice may offer some insights and nuance.
1943
The first artificial neural networks were proposed; abstract machines modeled after biological neurons (brain cells).
1958

Frank Rosenblatt built a relatively simple neural network that can be trained, called the perceptron.

It can trained through examples. This idea of learning by example has been the standard for learning machines since then.

Following the hype of the perceptron and its shortcomings (it was too simple) is the long AI Winter(s).
1969
Artist Sol LeWitt started doing something called wall drawings, sets of instructions that produce abstract drawings. These can be thought of as precursors to generative art.
"The idea becomes a machine that makes the art." (Paragraphs on Conceptual Art, 1967) It's not quite generative art yet but it's close. Besides, LeWitt called it something else: “conceptual art”.
1968
Vera Molnár brought us closer to modern generative art by actually using computers and writing code to generate artworks.

Yeah I know the timeline order is messed up, but the story makes more sense in this order.
1998

Since 1998, artists began meeting in generative art conferences and the artistic community started converging on a shared definition of generative art.

Generative art — work that has been produced by the execution, usually by a computer system, of a set of rules or procedures determined by the artist.<
2013

Deep learning revolution: The intersection of advancements in computing power, improved machine learning algorithms, and huge ever-growing datasets of digital things resulted in bigger and better neural networks. The great potential of machine learning and AI was reignited.

Buffed image recognition, picture captioning, language translation, text-to-speech, etc.

Advancements in generative networks: Instead of processing an input into an output (e.g. image to label), generative networks can generate new data on its own.

This seems to be the start of the image synthesis wave.

Let me introduce you to no one. This is a fake person generated by a generative adversarial network (GAN) trained on portraits.

Oh, and this must be where the confusion starts. The term generative in machine learning does not relate to generative art, but through many tongues, somehow generative AI art now belongs to the same class as generative art.

2015

The first modern text-to-image model was introduced.

Text-to-image models are systems that use deep neural networks to generate images from natural language descriptions.

It was called alignDRAW.
2021

DALL·E was revealed by OpenAI. Another text-to-image model, one that caught the public eye.
2022

2022 saw the burst of NFTs, which have been associated with generative art.

Honestly, some of them are on the borderline shallow end of generative art, Mad Libs-style images generated from shufflings of pre-drawn hairstyles, sunglasses, and clothes. They are more akin to the musical dice game.
2022

Text-to-image models were approaching the quality of real images like photographs. People were using these image generators to generate artworks that approach the quality of human art.
2024

Today we see different tools to make generative art, from none (straight up coding), to frameworks like p5.js, to full-blown authoring software like TouchDesigner and Houdini.
